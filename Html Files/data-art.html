Data-Art

Asteroids aren't just space rocks; they have interesting names too! While planets have mythological names, asteroids are named after real people, places, or even fictional characters. This data-art dives into this cool fact, showing these asteroids as simple bubbles on your screen. It's not fancy, but it's a fun and educational way to explore their sizes, speeds, distances, and names. Each bubble represents a different asteroid, making it a cool visual representation of some pretty complex info. The sizes of the bubbles represent the sizes of the asteroids and the colours of the bubbles represent the absolute magnitude(brightness) of the asteroids. So, take a look and discover the universe in a whole new light!

Data-Art(Exam)

"Did you know that the largest known asteroid in our solar system is Ceres? It's also classified as a dwarf planet and resides in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter." As for the art piece involving asteroids, imagine an interactive visualization that showcases the orbits and characteristics of various asteroids. Users can engage with this piece by clicking and dragging to move each asteroid and hovering over individual asteroids to reveal detailed information about their name, size, speed, and absolute magnitudes. Through this interactive experience, users can explore the dynamic nature of asteroids, learn about their diversity, and comprehend their trajectories within the solar system. This engagement allows users to explore and comprehend the fascinating world of asteroids in an informative and captivating manner.
